Common questions

Selling in Portugal, answered.

How much is my home in Portugal worth?

It depends on location, condition, and what comparable homes are actually closing for, not the asking prices you see online. I prepare a free, data-backed valuation using recent local sales and current demand, so you start from a realistic, defensible number.

What does it cost to sell a property in Portugal?

The main costs are the agency commission (typically a percentage of the sale price, plus VAT), a valid energy certificate, and, depending on your situation, capital gains tax on any profit. I give you a clear net-proceeds estimate up front so you know what you'll actually walk away with.

Do I need an energy certificate to sell?

Yes. A valid energy certificate (Certificado Energético) is legally required before you can advertise or complete the sale of a property in Portugal. I arrange a certified assessor so this is ready, not a last-minute scramble.

Will I pay capital gains tax when I sell?

Possibly, it depends on whether you're a Portuguese tax resident, whether it was your main home, and when you bought. Relief can apply when reinvesting the proceeds from a main residence. The rules are specific, so I work alongside a tax adviser and give you an estimate before you list.

Can I sell my Portuguese property from abroad?

Yes. With a power of attorney I can manage viewings, paperwork and even the notary signing on your behalf, and keep you updated by video and email throughout, you don't need to fly in for every step.
